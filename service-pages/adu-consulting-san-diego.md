## ADU Financing Options for Vista Homeowners

### Option 1: Cash-Out Refinance
If you have significant home equity and current interest rates are favorable, refinance your primary mortgage and pull out cash for ADU construction.

**Pros:** 
- Lowest interest rates (typically 1-2% less than construction loans)
- Single monthly payment

**Cons:**
- Resets your mortgage timeline
- May not make sense if current rate is very low

### Option 2: Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)
Revolving credit line secured by your home equity. Draw funds as needed during construction.

**Pros:**
- Only pay interest on what you use
- Flexibility for phased construction

**Cons:**
- Variable rates (can increase)
- Requires good credit (typically 680+)

### Option 3: Construction Loan
Short-term loan that converts to permanent financing after ADU completion.

**Pros:**
- Interest-only during construction
- Designed specifically for this purpose

**Cons:**
- Higher rates than traditional mortgages
- Two-step process (construction then refinance)

### Option 4: Cash Payment
If you have liquid assets, paying cash avoids interest and speeds up the process.

**Pros:**
- No loan approval delays
- No interest costs

**Cons:**
- Opportunity cost of capital
- Depletes savings

**We connect you with Russell Cox**, our preferred lender, who specializes in ADU financing and can analyze which option optimizes your situation.

## Vista ADU Regulations: What You Need to Know (2026)

**State ADU Law Highlights:**
- **No owner-occupancy requirement** (as of 2025)
- **Ministerial approval** for compliant ADUs (no discretionary review)
- **Reduced setbacks:** 4' side/rear allowed in most cases
- **No parking requirements** if within 0.5 mile of transit
- **ADU + JADU allowed** on most single-family lots

**Vista-Specific Requirements:**
- **Maximum size:** 1,200 SF for detached ADUs
- **Height limit:** Typically 16-18' depending on zoning
- **Lot coverage:** Combined structures usually can't exceed 40-50% of lot
- **Design standards:** Exterior materials should complement primary home

**HOA Restrictions:**
Even with state ADU laws, some Vista HOAs still impose restrictions. We help you:
- Review CC&Rs for ADU clauses
- Navigate HOA approval process
- Leverage state law when HOAs overstep authority

## Frequently Asked Questions: ADUs in Vista

**Q: How long does it take to build an ADU in Vista?**
A: From initial consultation to move-in: typically 10-14 months (2-3 months planning/permits, 6-10 months construction).

**Q: Do I need to live on the property to build an ADU?**
A: No. As of 2025, California eliminated owner-occupancy requirements for ADUs.

**Q: Can I sell my ADU separately from my main house?**
A: Generally no. ADUs are legally tied to the primary parcel. However, recent SB 9 legislation allows lot splits in some cases, potentially enabling separate sale. We can evaluate your property for this option.

**Q: Will an ADU raise my property taxes?**
A: Yes, slightly. Property tax will increase based on the added value (ADU construction cost), but it's typically modest compared to the rental income or value gain.

**Q: What's the difference between an ADU and a JADU?**
A: A Junior ADU (JADU) is a smaller unit (500 SF max) created within the existing primary home's footprint (often a bedroom conversion). JADUs have more restrictions but lower costs. Many Vista lots can accommodate both an ADU and JADU.

**Q: Can I use my ADU for short-term rentals (Airbnb)?**
A: Vista regulations on short-term rentals vary by neighborhood. We'll help you research current rules and obtain necessary permits/licenses if allowed.
